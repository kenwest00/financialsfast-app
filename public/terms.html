4. Output Accuracy and Limitations

The P&L statements generated by the Service are based on automated transaction classification using artificial intelligence. While we design our classification system for high accuracy, we cannot guarantee that every transaction will be correctly categorized. Specific limitations include:

  • Cash-basis only. All P&L statements are generated on a cash basis. Revenue is recorded when deposited and expenses when paid. No accrual adjustments are applied.
  • Transaction description dependency. Classification accuracy depends on the quality of transaction descriptions provided by your bank. Vague or abbreviated descriptions may result in misclassification.
  • Single-account limitation. The current version processes one bank account per P&L. Multi-account consolidation is not yet available.
  • Estimates and assumptions. Certain items (vehicle expense proration, loan interest/principal splits, depreciation) are estimated based on information you provide and standard assumptions. These estimates may differ from actual figures.
  • No independent verification. We do not independently verify the information in your bank statements or questionnaire responses.

We strongly recommend having a qualified accountant review any P&L statement before submitting it for tax filings or regulatory purposes. For loan applications, most lenders accept owner-prepared financial statements, but you should confirm with your specific lender.
